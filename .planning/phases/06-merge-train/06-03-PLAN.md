<objective>
Close the final Phase 6 gap: make the `feature_integration_failed` handler
symmetric with `feature_integration_complete` by calling
`conflicts.releaseCrossFeatureOverlap` after `features.failIntegration`.

Without this change, any secondary feature whose `runtimeBlockedByFeatureId`
points to a primary that fails integration remains stranded â€” its tasks stay
suspended and nothing unblocks them. This is the Pitfall 3 identified in RESEARCH
and noted in the 06-02 summary handoff.

Two concrete deliverables:
1. **Symmetric failure-path release** in `events.ts`. After `features.failIntegration`,
   call `await conflicts.releaseCrossFeatureOverlap(event.featureId)` and process
   the results identically to the success handler (`repair_needed` and `blocked`
   outcomes â†’ `features.createIntegrationRepair`).
2. **Tests** at unit and integration level proving secondaries are not silently
   starved after a primary integration failure.

This plan also serves as the Phase 6 acceptance gate: after these tests pass,
all four ROADMAP success criteria for Phase 6 are covered end-to-end.

Purpose: Satisfies SC4 ("Two features with cross-feature conflicts handled via
conflict coordination protocol, not silent starvation"). Also satisfies the
observability requirement from CONTEXT â€” cross-feature protocol is not complete
until it is provable in tests.

Output: One modified source file (`events.ts`), two extended test files.
No new migrations, no new modules, no config changes.
</objective>
